ESOL

For specific course information follow the links below:

Entry Level 1|Entry Level 2|Entry Level 3


ESOL classes enable learners to improve their skills in the areas of:

  • Speaking and Listening
  • Reading
  • Writing
A range of classes are available from pre-entry level.

Course Structure

Speaking and Listening classes enable learners to improve their skills in:
  • Listening and responding, speaking to communicate and engaging in discussions
Reading classes enable learners to:
  • Read and understand, and to read and obtain information
Writing classes enable learners to:
  • Write to communicate and to improve their spelling, grammar and punctuation skills
Entry Requirements
All learners will need to complete an application which must be supported by evidence to confirm fee status. All learners must attend an interview and undertake an initial and diagnostic assessment and will then be offered a class at a suitable level.

Course Progression
Learners may progress from Pre-entry through the following levels:
Entry Level 1
Entry Level 2
Entry Level 3

Progression to higher levels available where appropriate.

Course Career Prospects
Improving speaking, listening, reading and writing skills will enable learners to access mainstream college provision.

If you are unsure about your progression pleasecontact us.

Information & Communications Technolgy (ICT)

We are running NVQ Level 1 and NVQ Level 2 courses in IT. We have accreditation for Diploma courses as well.

These days, computers are everywhere. So it stands to reason that improving your computer skills is one sure way to get ahead. Our centre offers a relaxed and supportive environment for you to learn in.

The course consists of the following main topics:

  1. Use IT systems
  2. Operating a computer
  3. IT trouble-shooting for users
  4. IT Maintenance For Users
  5. It security for users
  6. Health & Safety in an IT Environment
  7. PC hardware
  8. Use IT to exchange information
  9. Internet and Intranet
  10. E-mail
  11. MS Word
  12. MS Excel
  13. Artwork and imaging software
  14. Database Software
  15. Presentation Software
  16. Website Software
  17. Specialist or bespoke software
  18. Impact of IT in our daily life.

Level 2 consists of MS Access as well.

National Certificate in Health and Social Care

This qualification is suitable for those studying in preparation in preparation for employment in Health and Social Care sector particularly in job where they will be expected to communicate effectively with people and provide active support within environment such as Care settings. The qualification is also suitable to support further studies in FE and HE. The qualification has been accredited into the National Framework (NVQ at level 2).

Entry Requirements: There are no formal entry requirements for this qualification. Suitable for both young 18+ es and for more mature students wishing to make a fresh start on a course that will prepare them for further learning or work involving health and social care related activities.

Qualification Structure: To achieve this qualification candidates must complete a total of six units consisting of four mandatory units and two optional units.

Duration: This will normally be one academic year.

Assessment and Grading: All units are centre-assessed and externally moderated by OCR. All units are equally weighted. To achieve the OCR Level 2 National Certificate in Health and Social Care, candidates must achieve a minimum pass grade for the four mandatory units and the two optional units � drawn from a bank of 15 units.

If a candidate is not able to complete a full qualification, their achievement will be recognised through the issue of Unit certificates listing the Units achieved. OCR recommended Text Book can be purchased online or from any of the notable bookshops.

Staff Experience / Expertise: All our staff are suitably qualified and trained as required by the qualifications body e.g. OCR, CACHE, City & Guilds etc.
